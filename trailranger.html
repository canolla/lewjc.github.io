TrailRanger

Final Project

UX/UI

Work In Progress

In recent years, the disconnection between young adults and the natural world has deepened as our lives increasingly revolve around technology. The fast pace of modern life—dominated by work, school, and other commitments can make it easy to overlook the profound benefits of nature, which offers not only physical exercise but also a space for reflection and mindfulness.

Outdoor activities like walking or hiking provide a much-needed break from screens, offering fresh air, sunlight, and movement that promote both wellbeing and physical health benefits. Thats where TrailRanger comes in. TrailRanger is an application which aims to help inspire and facilitate outdoor exploration through gamification. The app targets where the target audiences attention is (technology) and aims to help connect young adults with the outdoors.

01 Research

For this project it was very important to gather some concepts and ideas which had been established with academically strong foundations. There were two general areas I was interested in covering, firstly the proposed problem, in relation to how young adults were losing connection with nature, and the potential nature and outdoor activities have to benefit them. And secondly, the effectiveness of using gamification and intrinsic motivation as methods to help inspire and influence changes in behaviour in a positive way.

Looking at the core problem, I found from a report conducted on university students across Australia and New Zealand, 72% of students experience stress weekly, primarily from balancing academic work with other responsibilities. I also found another study which targeted young adults aged 18-24, finding that 58% of respondents stated being in nature and understanding what's around them is one of the biggest aids to helping them destress. Despite this nearly half want to be outside more but can't find appealing activities.

To gain some more insights into the target audience, I conducted a survey. I discovered that 89% of respondents would like to spend more time outdoors exploring, and that fitness and exploration were the two most common motives for going outdoors among respondents.

From this research, among other insights, it was clear to me that young adults are stressed and find it harder to connect with nature than in times when nature was less prevalent. What was also clear is that nature provides a large list of benefits to young adults lives and wellbeing and that finding a way to inspire and make this more accessible was crucial to helping with nature reconnection.

To try to answer this I delved into intrinsic motivation and gamification. I discovered a few key concepts, one of the core concepts was self-determination theory which offered a framework for understanding human motivation. It focuses on three fundamental psychological needs: autonomy, competence and relatedness. These needs, when fulfilled, create environments that foster intrinsic motivation, engagement, and personal growth.

I also delved into the Octalysis Framework, an important framework designed to help with understanding and implementing gamification strategies. The Octalysis Framework is framed around 8 core human drives as shown above. In this project, these core drives are instrumental in developing features that deeply engage users and provide them with rewarding experiences, encouraging meaningful progress within the app and
increased interaction with nature.

02 Visual Identity

The colour palette was established, taking inspiration from existing topographical maps. I researched topographic map designs and sampled a variety of colours that contrast well with each other.

The colours are bright yet soft, green is used as the primary accent colour while the other accent colours are utilised throughout the award designs.

The typography was carefully selected to provide a clean, minimal, yet characterised look, The Projekt Blackbird font offers an interesting contrast to the body font Aspekta.

The logo and display text is where Projekt Blackbird is implemented while Aspekta is implemented in a majority of UI and body text across different touchpoints.

This is a pattern which was used as part of TrailRanger's brand identity. it was created in p5.js representing differential line growth which is a phenomena seen throughout nature such as in leaves, fingerprints, fruits and even the shape of brains.

It's used within the TrailRanger brand as a metaphor for connecting with nature and exploring different paths.

03 UX/UI

I began the UX process by empathising with the target audience by creating personas and user journeys establishing their pain points and their desires.

I then developed a sitemap and began prototyping lofi’s and mid-fi's to gain a better understanding of
the app layout.

Finally, I designed the final app. Above is the Explore user flow, where users can browse and discover different trails, finding out info like distance, time, etc., as well as if there are competitions or challenges available
there to participate in.

Then there's the Arena, which is the main gamified aspect of the app. It includes optimal challenges that adapt to users skill levels and push them slightly out of their comfort zones. It also includes competitions which feature different types of activities. The competition Summit Squads allows users to get into groups and compete for the fastest time to win achievements and to contribute towards their personal growth.

The Walk user flow provides users an easy way to track and log their walks.

The Community page allows users to connect with others and see what’s happening. Staying up to date with each other's walking activity, competitions, and achievements.

The profile page features users stats, xp (experience points) as well as a walk log, progress journey (a scrollable timeline of personal progress) and all achievements earned.

This is a shot of the personal progression journey, located in the profile page. Users can progress through levels via walking, completing challenges, and competing in competitions. Each level corresponds with an icon of a native creature to New Zealand, creating that local connection to the creatures that reside throughout New Zealand.
